# **Conclusion of Analysis**

### The analysis reveals stark anomalies in India's crime conviction data across multiple dimensions. In West Bengal and Mizoram, the number of rape convictions suspiciously exceeds those for assault, suggesting severe under-reporting of lesser sexual offences. Several states like Gujarat show a complete collapse in riot convictions alongside stable or rising murder rates, possibly indicating the reclassification of communal violence. The prison system analysis identifies Meghalaya and Puducherry as facing the most strain, marked by high and volatile growth in inmate populations. Additionally, only Delhi, Tamil Nadu, and Uttarakhand demonstrated the sustained specialized capacity needed to secure counterfeiting convictions every year over the five-year period. These patterns collectively point to systemic data quality issues and potential manipulation in crime statistics across the country.
